Student Loan Servicer Navient Recieves Over 2 Million Complaints in Lawsuit

By: Jack Clifton

The student loan servicer Navient, among the largest in the country, has been a part of a number of lawsuits in the last 6 years.

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), with the help of 39 U.S. State Attorney's General, filed a lawsuit in 2017 alleging, among other things, that Navient:

1. Failed to correctly apply or allocate borrower student loan payments to their accounts

2. Steered struggling borrowers toward multiple forbearances instead of income-driven repayment plans

3. Deceived private student loan borrowers about cosigner release requirements

4. Reported loans incorrectly to the credit bureaus

5. Failed to inform borrowers of IDR plan renewal deadlines

$1.7 billion in student loans will be forgiven, along with $95 million in restituion payments to 350,000 student loan borrowers. The restitution payments come out to about $260 per person. Navient will also pay the Attorney's General $142.5 Million, and implement new practices that are less predatory to those recieving loans.
